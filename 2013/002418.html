On 02/18/2013 11:45 AM, Kyle Sallee wrote:


> I have not built any new boxes in a few years,
> Consequently, I am woefully ignorant of newer things like GPT.

Yes, the only two issues moving things in this direction are large HDDs
or partitions and UEFI. Other than that no one needs it. Grub2 works
well with this gpt and Lilo can too, but one must mind the partition
sizes of / and or in combination with /boot.

It's actually exceedingly easy to do, but going from gpt to ms-dos type
MBR is kind of a pain unless you know the secret, LOL. It's only three
or four commands in parted but no intuitive.

Here's the docs I tend to follow when preparing formatting a drive
w/gdisk as gpt:
https://wiki.archlinux.org/index.php/Unified_Extensible_Firmware_Interface#Create_an_UEFI_System_Partition_in_Linux

The new wave of laptops and workstations bastardized by mACROsF0t
require a basic understanding of this. I've done it a few times with
Arch and once w/Slackware.

Again, if the box isn't a UEFI box then you don't have to use gpt disks
- - you shouldn't have any sort of need to mount / on partitions not
accessable by Lilo or fdisk.
