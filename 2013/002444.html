> > Just a quick pointer...
> >
> > there is a utility called cfdisk.... Just type cfdisk /dev/sda at the
> > command line. This utility will then look at device sda, and tell you
> > which partitions are on it. It will probably be your windows disk, the
> > names of the partitions will make that clear.
> >
> > Still, you will be able to see the sizes of the partitions as well.
> > Quit, then try cfdisk /dev/sdb... this will tell you the partitions on
> > device sdb, which will most probably be the smaller disk.
> >
> > To get dual booting working properly, I have used easyBCD. It's USD20
> > windows program, but it allows windows 7 to hibernate properly. Only
> > drawback is that every time Sorcerer updates the bootblock, you will
> > need to re-install the boot block for Sorcerer in Windows. I have a
> > small script that does this automatically from Sorcerer, but it's
> > specific to my box and might destroy the partitioning on yours.... I'm
> > still not quite home yet, so I am not even in front of my desktop which
> > does the dual-booting.
