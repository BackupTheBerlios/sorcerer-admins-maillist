If a post bounces, then trim off the quoted back text and then re-submit a
message.

The 64K message limit exists for several reasons,
for one it encourages patch files to be compressed and sent directly
to the person who wants to receive it.
And spells should be attached xz compressed tarballs and in that state not
exceed 64K.
However, the messages I type can be close to the RAM limit of a Commodore
64 computer.  :)
Therefore, SAs are sometimes encouraged to do selective commenting
which means to delete quoted back text that is not relevant and then add
comments directly below quoted back text that it pertains to.
Typically, I neither care nor rebuke anyone for doing one style or another.
When Evert and I are discussing potential implementation changes then
we tend to do selective quote back and inline responses just below.
But otherwise we might either do no quote backs or top posting.
Top posting seems to be what gmail does by default if those 3 dots are not
clicked.

Having previous conversations with > at the beginning of each line provided
in a new message
is a nfity and relatively new  feature in the world of email clients.
I already take it for granted.
However, I do not remember old email clients that used POP3 and IMAPS such
to retrieve
such as the programs pine and mutt, and SMTP to send; providing courtesy
quote back.
Yet the down side is that unnecessary quoted back text consumes Internet
bandwidth.
Consequently, the  64K limit is not in order to be compassionate to those
SAs
that are running Modern Magic on Commodore 64 computers.  *chuckles*  As
if.  ;)
It is merely a reasonable sanity limit,
because mail sent through a list becomes bandwidth required multiplied
by all of the people who are subscribed to the list.
