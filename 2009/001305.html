The update was tested already on the test box a workstation.
The IA32 and x86_64 I/R are being updated now.
It seems to be updating smoothly.

However, I am concerned that there might yet occur a problem
with some sources that have circular requirements.
There was a little something like that having to do with
gtk+ uim and scim, but I saw an easy way around it.
However, this update is probably
the most complicated update to happen in years,
because when a relatively low level library was removed
it caused an unexpected hole in libtool archive files.

Pkgconfig files, libtool archive files, ELF libraries,
and dynamically linked installed software must all fit together properly.
I expect that it is too much of a headache for
system admins and distribution maintainers to check and fix it all.
Fortunately, sorcery does a purty good job of it
even with boxes that have unique software loadouts and
installed custom spells.
It is a nice coverage that demonstrates why sorcery is
better than a package manager when a SA tends to have
custom software installed.

But if something does go unexpectedly wrong with the update
I want SAs to know where to look first.
That is why I posted information about an update
that might probably look a bit unusual.
