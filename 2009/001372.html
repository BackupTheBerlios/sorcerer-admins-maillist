More info and a partial solution:

The sound problem affects only certain applications or sound formats, not sure 
which is correct. For example,  amarok2 would not play any music and display 
instead a message on start up 'Sound device hw0,0 does not work'. Any attempts 
to configure use of a different sound device provided the same outcome. 

It was interesting however that the KDE sound configuration dialog (the same 
one is opened by amarok2) offers a 'Test' button which actually plays sound!

I have recompiled qt-x11-opensource and the problem persisted.

Then, I have installed the phonon 4.3.1 spell again and recompiled amarok2. 
Voila! Amarok now plays music again. 

Note that the phonon spell is marked broke and that the 4.3.1 version is not 
available on the KDE servers anymore, at least I could not find it. 
Fortunately, I have found a copy of the 4.3.1 phonon sources on my computer...
