This email is information rather than useful and can be disregarded.


lzip is yet another interface for creating LZMA compressed files.
However, unlike lzma, lzip does provide a magic at the front.
Also it accepts the same command line parameters as gzip,
and also a few extra lzma specific parameters.
And it can decompress concatenated lzip compressed files.
Seems great so far right?

However, it compresses twice as slow as 7za from p7zip.
The lzma compression by lzip is not quite as tight as 7za.
And there might not be a zlib type library for lzip.
It was looking very good until I discovered that.

There is not yet an ideal lzma drop in replacement for gzip.
For now lzip has some conveniences that 7za lacks,
yet 7za has speed and efficiency that lzip lacks.
Therefore, Sorcerer will not be switching support away from 7za and to lzip.
Instead sorcery and sorcery-bgz were modified
to support both 7za compressed tarballs and lzip compressed tarballs.

We might start to see some lzip compressed source tarballs.
There are lzma alone compressed source tarballs.
However,  I do not write spells that download lzma alone compressed tarballs,
because lzma alone lack an identifiable magic at the front.
