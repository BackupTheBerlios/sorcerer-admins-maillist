Hi there .

I, for one am glad that someone else is actually trying to install Sorerer
from the I/R and running in to a few issues.
Without trying to flame, I would like to add my bit.

The installer does need a little work. We should all test it a little more
and give more feedback to Kyle in order to get it in a good enough shape to
compete with the other installers out there.

I agree that the Sorcerer I/R should be as small as possible. In my mind, as
long as you have a working Sorcerer that is network aware, you can cast
anything you want. This approach has the added benefit of only installing
what you need.

I do not agree with having the option to select any spell for which you do
not have sources until Sorcerer is installed and network aware.

Lastly, by avoiding the skillcheck of compiling a custom kernel, we now have
a much bigger skillcheck when trying to do anything a little different with
the install, like a virtual environment, installing on removable media like
usb hard drives. There should, at the very least be some clear guidelines on
how to go from linux-modular to linux. Since I am the person that managed
this a few times, I'll try to re-install and at least write this little bit
of documentation.


I wish you all a very nice day.
-Evert Vorster-
