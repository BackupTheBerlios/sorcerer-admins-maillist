bash 4.0 was recently released.
It will be headed to your boxes soon.
I already started playing with some of the new functionality of bash 4.0
Consequently, I started an unstable branch of sorcery.
Naturally, it requires bash 4.0 to be installed for it to work properly.

Some changes I made to sorcery unstable made it faster,
yet in other ways slower.
Achieving an overall performance increase may be difficult.
Therefore, do not necessarily expect that sorcery unstable versions
will be better or faster than sorcery stable.

One of the nifty new things bash can do is associative arrays.
Perl programmers are familiar with those.
It is an array where the subscripts are not necessarily numbers, but
can be words.
Examples:

DOG[doberman]="Menacing bark"
DOG[chiwawa]="Yo quiero taco bell"


A recent deluge of updates pushes the next release of the I/R further away.
Also many installed spells will be ELFed up due to the readline update.
As usual those spells will be detected and recompiled.
