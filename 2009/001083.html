On Sun, Apr 19, 2009 at 11:37 PM, evert vorster <evorster at gmail.com> wrote:
> How about just having an attribute in spells that says compile or run-time?
> That way an SA could dispel $(augur attribute $ATTRIBUTE) and in this case,
> get rid of all the spells that are needed for compiling, but not needed for
> running.

It might work.
However, some spells might be runtime while others might be build.
It is not always a clear distinction.
gcc, for example, is both.
Some sources install both an executable and a library.
Sometimes the library is required during compilation.
Sometimes it is not.
Sometimes the executable is required during runtime.
Sometimes it is required during compiltion.
Therefore, only some spells such as "make"
fit the attribute of being a build-only requirement.
However, almost no spell requires make
since make is assumed to be installed already
on Sorcerer boxes.
