On Mon, Dec 7, 2009 at 5:55 AM, Kyle Sallee <kyle.sallee at gmail.com> wrote:

> I was researching some new stuff to make sysinit more smooth
> and free it from the possibility of a buggy udev causing the mounting
> of the real root filesystem to become impossible.
> Something I discovered was that grub supports loading multiple
> cpio initramfs images per kernel while lilo only supports one.
> If lilo could handle at least 2 per kernel image
> then that would allow us to conserve some space on /boot/
>
> My reservation about grub is the same as usual.
> What is (hd0,0)?
> Is that the first partition of the first SCSI drive?
> Is that the first partition of the first EIDE drive?
> From the point of view of the menu driven installer
> how should it determine the proper sequence of
> translation from traditional /dev names for devices
> to grub names for devices?
>
>
>
> Also I found some interesting make targets for linux
> that I did not previously notice.
>
>  localmodconfig  - Update current config disabling modules not loaded
>  localyesconfig  - Update current config converting local mods to core
>
> A bit too dangerous to be useful with the linux* spells in the grimoire,
> but for those people thinking about transitioning from a modular linux
> to a tiny monolithic kernel or just a tinier custom kernel
> then one of those make targets might be useful.
> Of course then you are on your own for maintaining and updating
> your .config if you do so.
