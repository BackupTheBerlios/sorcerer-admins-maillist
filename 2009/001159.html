It might have created a log in /var/log/
that explains why the initramfs was regenerated.
A change in /etc /lib /lib64 /bin /sbin /boot
could cause the initramfs to regenerate.
A change in /etc/sorcery/ would not necessarily
cause the initramfs to regenerate.
However, it regenerates as required.
Generally, it is better that it is done,
rather than have to trouble the SA to do it manually.

There may be a bug in bash that effects initramfs regeneration.
If /etc/lilo.conf.tail does not exist.
# touch /etc/lilo.conf.tail

That will cause the initramfs to regenerate.
But after that the bash bug involving the absence of that file
should not cause the initramfs to regenerate when not required.
I have compared the relevant code to the bash manual page.
