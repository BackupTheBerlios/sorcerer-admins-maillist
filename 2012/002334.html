Hi there...

As Kyle said, 3000+ spells is a lot to keep track of, and as long as a
spell works, it sticks around, even if its a little old.

Demetri, from your description of the problem, it sounds like you have a
video driver problem.
If you give us your hardware configuration, I might be able to give you
some pointers on how to set it up.

When you see the blinking cursor, Hold down "Ctrl" and hit "C"

You should now be able to log in.
Enter lspci and hit "Enter"

This will give you a list of what hardware is in your system.
If you feel like troubleshooting this issue for yourself, read:

/var/log/Xorg.0.log
See what modules are being loaded to get your X going, and where it is
failing.

You can edit /etc/X11/xorg.cfg, but please read the manual on that first,
by running man xorg.cfg

You can test various configurations by running "startx" When successful,
you will get a very basic window manager instead of that blinking cursor.
If you get the cursor again, you get to try again, free of charge.

When you finally have a working X config, exit out of the window manager by
either pressing "Ctrl" and "Backspace" together, or typing "exit" into the
left-most terminal.

Once you exit there, you can start the proper window manager by entering:
/etc/init.d/kdm restart
and hitting Enter.

Good luck.
