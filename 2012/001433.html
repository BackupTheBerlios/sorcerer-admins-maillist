to my todo list about how I aught to change the menu driven installer<br>
in order to create a greater probability of a successful installation.<br>
So your feedback will be of great help to future Sorcerer SAs.<br>
Sorcerer might never become a noob friendly distro,<br>
because the price for wielding so much power<br>
is of course the knowledge and skill required to do so.<br>
However, Sorcerer still has a long evolution ahead towards<br>
achieving better between the power / knowledge requirement ration.<br>
If that ratio is now 1/1 then in the future 100/1 would be even better.<br>
A means to that end, to shifting it in that direction<br>
is to improve the usability of the menu driven installer.<br>
<br>
Currently, the menu driven installer is a bit like a skill check.<br>
In the past it definitely was a skill check.<br>
It required the SA to complete a successful make menuconfig<br>
on a linux kernel.<br>
To be blunt most experienced SAs<br>
were unable to pass the skill check.<br>
They would manage to install it,<br>
but be unable to boot it<br>
because they did something like:<br>
forgetting to compile drivers for their fixed disk;<br>
making those fixed disk drivers modular instead of compiled in;<br>
forgetting to compile the filesystems they used for installing;<br>
forgetting to make those filesystems compiled in;<br>
or for other reasons;<br>
<br>
Today's menu driven installer is much much much easier.<br>
Sorcerer comes with a pre-compiled modular kernel.<br>
And upon updates of boxes the modular kernel is<br>
configured automatically and compiled.<br>
So the SA is no longer troubled to have the skills that<br>
are required in order to have a custom linux kernel.<br>
This was a HUGE skill check.<br>
And now it is gone.<br>
<br>
The fact that the menu driven installer has no apparent bugs<br>
and works, yet it is not a successful experience for everyone<br>
is an indication that it is still a skill check.<br>
It is the gate that bars the entry of some SAs.<br>
Yet, it is not the intention of Sorcerer designers to prohibit<br>
anyone from using the distro.<br>
So having such a high, thick iron gate at the front<br>
is not intentional, but it is a a consequence of how<br>
the menu driven installer is designed.<br>
<br>
The menu driven installer is designed to grant SAs<br>
nearly all the freedom that a SA would have doing a manual install,<br>
yet with the convenience of not having to type those commands.<br>
To an experienced SAs the menu driven installer is like<br>
several trails of bread crumbs.<br>
The SA must only decide which bread the SA likes best<br>
and then follow that trail.<br>
But those trails are invisible to noob SAs<br>
that are unfamiliar with what is required in order to install a distro.<br>
This is something that was not given consideration<br>
when the menu driven installer was created.<br>
The main consideration for the menu driven installer<br>
is whether it will allow senior level SAs to do what senior<br>
level SAs would want to do during an installation.<br>
And yes, it can do LUKS encryption, LVM2, software raids, etc...<br>
So it seems like a success.<br>
But it is also a successful portcullis, unfortunately.<br>
<br>
If someone continues trying<br>
then they will definitely achieve success with the installer.<br>
But I can not know if the experience of Sorcerer for a noob<br>
is any better than would be their experience using a distro<br>
that is designed for noobs.<br>
One should not make the premature conclusion that all<br>
distros are as difficult to install as Sorcerer.<br>
Distros that are designed for noobs should be easier to install.<br>
There are even "Live CD" distros that can be booted from CD<br>
and used without having to install to a fixed disk.<br>
Each type of distro has different strengths and weaknesses.<br>
<br>
Sorcerer is very far away from the center or norm for distros.<br>
Sorcerer caters very heavily to senior level SAs' requirements,<br>
needs and feedback.<br>
Sorcerer has all the classic strengths and weaknesses<br>
of a true pure source based distro.<br>
Sorcerer is not like a hybrid distro that tries to be a blend<br>
of a package and source based distro.<br>
For people who are happy with the advantages of Sorcerer<br>
and not bothered by the disadvantages<br>
then Sorcerer is an excellent and yes even fun tool!<br>
But for a noob I would imagine that Sorcerer is probably<br>
closer to a reoccurring nightmare,<br>
or at least one bucket of cold sweat.<br>
That is not to imply that Sorcerer is lame.<br>
Sorcerer is merely awesome for what Sorcerer was designed to do.<br>
<br>
I hope you decide to continue trying and<br>
achieve success with installing and deploying Sorcerer.<br>
I am told that by SAs that using Sorcerer for 2 months<br>
is a more educational experience than using a package<br>
based distro for 2 years.<br>
Actually, the person who last told me it gave a name of<br>
the distro he used before and said 2 weeks instead of 2 months,<br>
but I do not want to to brag about Sorcerer<br>
nor put down any specific distro.<br>
<br>
Each distro is basically like a tool.<br>
Some are like flat head screw driver.<br>
Others are like a hammer.<br>
Selecting the right tool for a task tends<br>
to achieve easy and desirable success.<br>
Yes, using a hammer to drive screws into a wall is a possibility.<br>
But one would not expect ideal results from the effort.<br>
Likewise driving a nail into a wall using a screw driver is possible.<br>
However, clearly that is a task best suited for a hammer.<br>
<br>
The 3 factors that merge and form success are:<br>
1. SA skill<br>
2. Distro design<br>
3. Role of the box.<br>
<br>
Clearly when the SA has great skill<br>
then skill is the most important factor.<br>
It also tends to be the limiting factor as<br>
to what distros the SA can select and deploy.<br>
Also there are some distros that are designed<br>
to do only one task such as to be deployed as a firewall.<br>
Using a role specific distro for a role specific task<br>
tends to produce good results for that task<br>
while usually requiring minimal effort and skill.<br>
<br>
Clearly SA skill is the most important factor,<br>
especially when a senior level SA is retrospecting.<br>
The limits of what can be accomplished using<br>
the sorcery tools is proportional to the SA's skill,<br>
especially the SA's skill with shell commands and shell scripting.<br>
<br>
The design of the sorcery tools are a modular set of building blocks.<br>
And a SA is like a young person fitting the building blocks together<br>
in order to create something amusing and interesting.<br>
The building blocks themselves look unassuming<br>
and uninteresting when in a pile and when not fitted together.<br>
The skill of the person putting the pieces together<br>
determines the grandeur of the final composition.<br>
And Sorcerer is very much like that.<br>
<br>
For example...<br>
Let us say that a SA is curious to know what<br>
10 installed software projects are consuming the most disk space.<br>
There is no single command to accomplish this.<br>
However, it can be assessed very quickly.<br>
<br>
# augur installed | augur size | sort -gr | head -n 10<br>
<br>
augur installed: that outputs a list of installed spells<br>
<br>
augur size: reads from stdin a list of spells<br>
 =A0 =A0 and outputs the kilobytes totals for installed files by that spell=
<br>
<br>
sort -gr: sorts input numerical and<br>
 =A0 =A0 then reverses the sort to output highest values first.<br>
<br>
head -n 10: prints the first 10 lines.<br>
<br>
Putting commands together like that is simple for a senior level SA.<br>
It is no different than stacking together some building blocks.<br>
And now it looks like something cool and useful. =A0:)<br>
<br>
This is an example that demonstrates that<br>
the ease at which sorcery can be wielded<br>
to achieve results and provide information<br>
is proportional to SA skill.<br>
To a new SA sorcery is probably somewhat confusing<br>
and perhaps not very interesting.<br>
But then again so were building blocks<br>
when I first saw them.<br>
At best I could make only a rectangular house.<br>
Not until many years later did someone show me<br>
how something that looked like a spaceship could<br>
be created from the building blocks.<br>
<br>
Sorry for the long explanation.<br>
But I hope it shows how sorcery and Sorcerer<br>
can be and can become a positive experience for a SA<br>
that has the tenacity required to become a senior level SA.<br>
Starting with something easier is possible.<br>
However, eventually, it will probably lead a SA<br>
back to something like Sorcerer eventually.<br>
<br>
My apology if that insinuates Sorcerer SAs are<br>
elite, snobbish, power mongers.<br>
To the contrary they are nice, helpful, and capable.<br>
Half of the time they tend to supply answers to<br>
to their own technical support questions.<br>
Even I enjoy receiving bug reports along with solutions.<br>
That is great for me since it saves me time and effort.<br>
<br>
Being a Sorcerer SA is both a great experience and<br>
also a bit like a badge of honor because to join us<br>
requires a rare combination of tenacity and/or skill.<br>
And yes, what one lacks in skill can be compensated<br>
with raw tenacity.<br>
Merely, trying all the paths that do not work eventually<br>
will lead a person to the path of success.<br>
I forget what the name for that is called.<br>
Success through perseverance perhaps?<br>
<br>
Making mistakes is not bad.<br>
Even by making a mistake one learns that<br>
is not an optimal method for achieving the desired result.<br>
So please grant yourself patience<br>
and enjoy the process of learning it<br>
even when making mistakes.<br>
<br>
To create the very first bootable Sorcerer I/R<br>
I did not copy nor look at what any other distro was doing.<br>
Instead I did some web searching to discover what software<br>
could be used as a bootloader on CDROM.<br>
I selected a few to try.<br>
Even upon deciding which one I wanted to use<br>
I did not achieve immediate success.<br>
Instead I spent nearly 5 days teaching myself how<br>
to successfully create a bootable CDROM image.<br>
Not everything comes immediately and easy.<br>
Even the current sorcery tools<br>
are a product of a decade's worth<br>
of use and feedback from SAs.<br>
It did not become awesome overnight.<br>
And the first rough drafts of the tools were<br>
little more than a technology demonstration<br>
that provided that source based management was possible.<br>
Unfortunately, most of the original design<br>
and original implementation had to be discarded<br>
due to short-sightedness and critical design flaws.<br>
That does seem to imply that it was a failure,<br>
but I prefer to think of it as a stepping stone on<br>
the path towards success.<br>
<br>
Sometimes success is closer than it might seem.<br>
But it can only be achieved by those with sufficient tenacity.<br>
<br>
The reason for that digression is to demonstrate<br>
that the high skill required in order to wield sorcery<br>
and install Sorcerer can be achieved through tenacity.<br>
Like many things in life,<br>
one can not fail at trying to becoming a Sorcerer SA<br>
unless one decides to quit trying.<br>
Personally, I feel that way about the Japanese language.<br>
Learning Japanese for an English speaker is very difficult.<br>
But as long as I do not quit trying<br>
then I have not yet failed.<br>
<br>
Tenacity is not merely that which separates senior level SAs<br>
from regular SAs, but also what separates most normal people<br>
from occupations like software engineer that require tenacity.<br>
I have met people that totally lack tenacity.<br>
They give into the slightest frustration<br>
and throw temper-tantrums.<br>
I feel sorry for those people because they move<br>
even simple tasks into the realm of impossibility.<br>
But that is a digression I do not want to continue to follow.<br>
<br>
Rather instead because you decided upon Sorcerer<br>
you are likely a person who has the required tenacity.<br>
In spite of what anyone might say to encourage you<br>
or discourage you, please remain confident in yourself.<br>
Remember the fable of "the tortoise and the hare."<br>
Even if slow moving,<br>
that which continues to push forward will eventually succeed,<br>
while those who have natural talent but lack tenacity<br>
will probably snooze and lose.<br>
