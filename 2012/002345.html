I do not intend to rush new I/R generation unless current I/Rs are unusable.
For the moment my todo list has plenty on it that I want to accomplish before
generating new I/Rs.
One of those things is to re-implement code to verify the content of the I/R
is properly intact since I do not know if SAs are properly verifying their
burned CDs using md5sum, dd, or cmp prior to attempting to use them
for installation on bare metal.
Additionally, sometimes a CD burned in one drive
might not read flawlessly in a different CD drive.
While unexpected I have seen it happen.

A mandatory CD integrity check will make installation slower,
but not by much since most boxes should encounter no difficulty
with caching about 200M of bytes from the CD.
I am pondering now whether to make it an init-script
or part of the menu driven installer.
If I waffle yet again on this
then only maple syrup will be necessary
to complete my breakfast
since I already quaffed a glass of orange juice.  :)

P.S.
Everyone actually checks their burned CDs before booting
them to verify integrity yes?
Transferring content to a USB SSD and
making it bootable is also a viable choice.
And a USB SSD will likely eliminate
almost all possibility of a read/write error.
The I/R does not mind being on USB SSD instead of CDROM.
The only requirement is that the device be recognized as removable.
