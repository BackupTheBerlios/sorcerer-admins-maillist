Well, if you manage this feat, you definitely do not deserve to be called
newb anymore.
Use the utility lspci to find out who made the wireless card that you have,
read as much as you can about it. I have an iwlwifi card in my laptop, and
can probably help you if you have something similar, or at least point you
in the right direction. It may just be that we don't have firmware for your
card yet, but with a good enough description, we can hack something
together that might work for you.

Unfortunately, that is only step 1. Actually connecting to a wireless

to find a GUI that works in KDE and can set up my wifi. I try about once a
year, and have given up in disgust a few times now.
I can tell you the steps I take, and Kyle might have his own take on
things, too.
