Now, as far as virtualization technologies are concerned. Many are good,
and some IMNSHO, are quite sucky - like OpenVZ, for an example of one
that sucks (No kernel no brain, no brain no pain no gain).

Xen is really good stuff. KVM is good stuff, VMware is good stuff, and
when discussing Oracle (EllisonCo) technologies inherited from other,
more noble predecessors, VirtualBox is pretty good on the desktop.

Their commercial offerings excel, as far as I'm concerned, on their
platforms, but such is a moot point due to the fact that we have ZFS on
Linux now, and I don't know when the last time you checked was, but
Oracle has a wonderful reputation for not returning their own customers
phone calls - even if they're under a support contract - so I doubt that
offering suggestions to them would be readily acknowledged.

Besides, Oracle's virtualization platforms don't scale well into the
layouts we professional Cloud and VPS providers can use as a profitable
model. It's hard enough to convince customers to spend the extra bucks
for VMware when all they need is a machine or machines that stay up and
perform flawlessly - Xen and KVM do that just fine and that's why almost
all providers use one or both of those technologies.
