> Sorcerer is differs from many distros.
> Sorcerer boxes are not completely re-installed
> each year from new I/Rs.
> For as long as a Sorcerer box's role is required
> the installed Sorcerer is updated periodically.
> The updates do not require new I/Rs.
> 
> Sorcerer boxes surf the wave of currently released free software.
> They update seamlessly from past to present to future.
> But they do not do super-long-jumps.
> And neither do pre-compiled binary based distros
> that require a re-install every year or so.
> 
> Semi-long jumps are possibly mainly on distros
> where the tools are not changing.
> Generally, the longer sorcery goes without major changes
> then the greater the span of time Sorcerer boxes can leap
> forward between updates.
> However, the sorcery tools are adapted
> to meet the changing challenges of
> downloading, compiling, and installing software.
> 
> The most recent major change was going from using
> a preloadble ELF library to track filesystem changes
> to using a DESTDIR type method of installation
> in order to track what files are installed when
> software finishes compiling.
> Sorcerer is a decade old.
> Support for DESTDIR has been gaining popularity
> and is common in almost all new software now.
> However, 5 years ago it was hardly in anything.
> 
> DESTDIR caught on faster than bzip2 did.
> Even today many sources are released as gzip
> compressed tarballs.
> However, a tiny fraction of software is now available
> as xz compressed tarballs.
> xz is an gzip like implementation of LZMA.
> the program supports gzip parameters
> and the compressed files have identifiable magic headers.
> And yes sorcery tools were updated to seamlessly
> recognize and support xz compressed tarballs
> just as it already supports gzip and bzip2 compressed tarballs.
> 
> I hope that somewhat explains the limitations of Sorcerer.
> Basically anything older than current is ancient history.
> Bug reports are only received on current grimorie spells,
> current sorcery, current I/Rs, etc...
