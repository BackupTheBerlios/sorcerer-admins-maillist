Hi there.

Welcome, again, to the world of linux. Unfortunately, you have had a bit of
a rough entry so far. If you have a lot of bandwidth, I would highly suggest
getting an ISO from a different distrobution, and install that to get a
"feel" for how things work in the UNIX world.

For ease of install, nothing beats Ubuntu. ( I try Ubuntu every year or so,
get annoyed at the horrible package manager, and drop it again )
Gentoo is a bit difficult to set up, and not as elegant as Sorcerer in quite
a few respects.
However, getting something like Sabayon would be a good second step, as it
has the power of Gentoo under the hood, and the ease of install that a
binary based distro offers.

If, on the other hand you really have a lot of time on your hands, and you
are not afraid of reading quite a few manual pages, then keep at it with
Sorcerer. I keep returning to Sorcerer just because of the power of the
tools, and the level of control I have over my computers. Sorcerer is
basically like LFS ( Linux From Scratch ), but with source management.
