> Hi Nathan :)
>
> Although I have been seeing this question over and over throughout the
> years, it is still, for some reason, one of the most common recurring
> questions.
>
> The absolute minimum would in fact be one. You can have a swap file
> instead of a swap partition, and you don't HAVE to have to mount /boot
> on its own partition, although a long discussion here last month pretty
> revealed that you actually 'should'.
>
> Look. make a partition for /boot of a Gbyte or so, and the rest for /
> and that should be fine for getting your sea legs.
>
> It's really no different than for any other UNIX, and once you find that
> you have a need for separate partitions to mount your /home, /var,
> /usr/local, RAID volumes, etc., then those will be mandatory per your
> requirements, as UNIX is as flexible as your imagination and experience
> dictates.
>
> Just install it with a partition for /boot and  / and take it, break it,
> and excel :)
>
> Hope that helps!
