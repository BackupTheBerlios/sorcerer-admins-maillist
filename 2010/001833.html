I'm of the same opinion as Jan on this Kyle. I use/depend upon
tcp_wrappers for things like NFS and portsentry, etc.

To me, it's kind of a 'standard' thing, expected to be part of a UNIX
installation, and used regularly.

I don't always use netfilter, often depending upon PfSense firewalls on
the perimeter, and quite often will just quickly enter some jerk into
/etc/hosts.deny (even on a LAN) for simplicity ;)

To do that w/iptables might require four or five rules, when I can do it
on a single line in /etc/hosts/deny for particular services.

wrt things like portsentry, I like the redundancy and have no problems
removing false positives from iptables AND /etc/hosts.deny as
a regular course of re-allowing access.

Not to mention its integral relationship to NFS. I'm hoping you'll
rethink your inclinations on this Kyle, and then we can all go back to
hating Redhat - coz it's fun! :)
