I am adjusting sorcery to generate more complete indexes.
That way sorcery can generate immediate answers for
arof, aarof, iron, aron and those type of inquiries
which involving requirements and lists of requirements.
Immediate is good, yes?

Previously, sorcery created indexes of immediate requirements and optionals.
When a complete requirement list for a spell was requested
then sorcery would assemble those lists.
arof and aarof were neither especially fast nor slow.
iron and aron requests were very slow.
And the functions that assembled requirement lists
were unusually complex.

The trade-off for immediate answers
is that generating the additional indexes requires more time.
On legacy boxes the wait will be noticed.
Modern boxes will barely notice an inconvenience for additional indexing time.

Probably the most visible aspect of this change
is that almost all of the wait time during sorcery
is consumed during indexing.
Most everything else about sorcery will run faster.
But nobody will notice the speed increase.
All that will be noticed is the wait during slow indexing.

The reason I am announcing this is
to answer in advance the inquiries about
why I made sorcery slower.
