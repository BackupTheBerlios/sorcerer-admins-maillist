> 
> Current Sorcerer I/Rs are available from
> http://sorcerer.silverice.org/download/iso9660/2010/
> Nothing older than the 20100529 is fixed or supported.
> Ancient Sorcerer I/Rs and those provided from magazines
> are collectors items and have no production value.
> If the I/R being used is not fresh
> then please download a new Sorcerer Install Rescue image.
> 
> After a Sorcerer box is deployed
> the box must be updated no less frequently
> than once per quarter.
> Updates are just updates, not complete re-installs.
> Once a Sorcerer box is installed the I/R is only
> useful for installing new boxes or rescuing existing boxes.
> 
> Yearly or bi-yearly please check for newer I/Rs
> Download and burn newer I/R images for the purpose
> of having a usable Rescue disk.
> Eventually each Sorcerer box will update to the point
> where an I/R used to install the box can no longer be
> used to rescue the box.
> 
> A Sorcerer box neglected and not updated
> for more than 1 year might not smoothly update.
> In that situation back up the important files
> and do a new install from a newer I/R
> unless the SA can hem the seams together.
> 
> Important transition points in sorcery development
> exist to provide backward compatibility for up to 6 months.
> So the path to a smooth update becomes overgrown
> and brambly after 6 months.
> 
> New grimoires are provided daily.
> Most boxes install a grimoire that is one day old.
> That is called the LAG grimoire.
> It also helps to make updates possible on boxes
> where the SA somehow failed to set the UTC date properly.
> Boxes that have an approximate day that is not exactly
> correct might update using the LAG=0 or LAG=2 grimoires.
> Having the date set properly for each box is important
> because that determines which grimoire they attempt
> to download.
> grimoires from previous years are not downloadable.
> Grimoires as old as 3 months and sometimes older
> are available for download.
> The grimoire is basically the catalog of software
> that is supported for easy installation.
> Software that does not yet have entries into the grimoire
> can be given entries by SAs, system administrators.
> Grimoire entries are called spells.
> Just as spelling defines the proper assembly of
> characters into a meaningful word
> grimoire spells contain the information for
> source acquisition, compilation and installation.
> They are very easy to understand
> and each box comes with more than 3000 example spells.
> 
> Hope that helps.
