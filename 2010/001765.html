Looks like the umount -lr / totally fails.
I was expecting it failed due to /proc umounting first,
but even after I grepped /proc out
the umount -lr for / was still failing to remount / read only.
Sure the umount -lr umounted the other filesystems
But it did not even remount / as read only, as it should.
So for lack of an explanation
of how to make this work properly
I did what might be dangerous.

Above the piped commands for umounting all filesystems
I added:
mount -o remount,ro /
That will either definitely ensure that / is remounted ro
and thus boot without a journal sync
or will hang a box during shutdown/restart
Multiple tests on the test box show that
the modified mount script successfully
remounts / as read only and thus expedites
booting by a second.  :)

I suppose none of us reboot our boxes enough
to notice that / was not always cleanly umounted.
But thanks to Roelof Wobben trouble error reporting
the problem will be fixed in the next LAG=0 grimoire.
