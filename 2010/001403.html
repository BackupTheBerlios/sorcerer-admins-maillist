Ah, I should add that...

I did checks with the initramfs to determine if /bin/pwd
is used during sysinit.
And it is not.
The easy way to tell is to do a standard boot.
It will make the /boot/fast/initrf
Then, I merely have to check that initramfs.
Since /bin/pwd was not included
that indicates it was never loaded/run during boot.

Having programs in /bin /sbin that will not be run during boot
or are not essential for rescue and repair
merely wastes space and makes booting longer than necessary.

So the the decision to move /bin/pwd to /usr/bin/pwd
was not in error nor a decision made without consideration.

There is no advantage for a source
to avoid using PATH to locate pwd.
Both /bin/ and /usr/bin/ would normally be in PATH.
