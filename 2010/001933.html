Probably that version is also buggy.
If physical volumes are not part of a volume group
and yet LVM2 blocks access to those devices
then I would consider that version of LVM2 to be buggy.
Running pvcreate on partitions used to be harmless
and was done as a convenience for SAs in some earlier
versions of the Sorcerer menu driven installer.
However, the most recent release of the Sorcerer I/R
requires that the SA select a menu option in order to
run pvcreate to mark a device as physical volume
and possible to include in a volume group.

Another way of putting this is that...
LVM2 only appears to be buggy on certain
installations of Sorcerer by certain Install/Rescue CDs.
I am not certain of which ones
and I do not have a distribution wide solution
for this potential problem.
However, /etc/init.d/vgchange can be made mode 600
on boxes that do not use logical volume management
and then LVM2 will not lock devices.
SAs must make that decision
since I do not want to decide by a sed or grep command
which boxes use LVM2 and risk disabling LVM2 start on
a box that has logical volumes.
