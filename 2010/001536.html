When running the installer in qemu
or a very slow emulator
then the creation of the menu in order
to select language locales
requires a considerable amount of time.
It could appear to hang for a few minutes.

I normally select something like
en.US-UTF8
and compile just a few locales.

After spell configuration is complete
then spell compilation and installation begins.
This can take between a few minutes
or an hour depending upon how one is doing it
and whether they selected an individual locale
or want to install all locales databases.

During the time when compilation is happen
an overview called
# augur scry
appears on the screen.
And this shows the progress of spells casting
and also what spells are waiting to cast.

After that completes the SA is returned
to the main menu of the menu driven installer.

Skipping the menus for the etc spell is fine.
The etc spell can always be cast at a later date
if the SA desires the convenience of learning about
what files in /etc/ should be edited and also being able
to read manual pages about those files.
