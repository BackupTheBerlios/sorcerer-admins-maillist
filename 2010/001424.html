Now, after you partition a disk.
The partitions will show on that menu.
But one must know what to look for.

Let us say that the disk device that was partitioned
was /dev/sda
Then the first partition of /dev/sda is /dev/sda1
The second partition of /dev/sda is /dev/sda2

If the device partitioned was /dev/hda
Then the first partition of /dev/hda is /dev/hda1
The second partition of /dev/hda is /dev/hda2

Additional IDE devices will have names such as:
/dev/hdb
/dev/hdc
/dev/hdd

Additional SCSI devices will have names such as:
/dev/sda
/dev/sdb
/dev/sdc

If they exist then they can be partitioned.
There is an exception of course.
An ATA based CDROM will have a device name such as
/dev/hdb or /dev/hdc or /dev/hdd
However, a cdrom device can not be partitioned.

A SCSI based CDROM has a device name such as
/dev/sg0 or /dev/scd0 /dev/scd1
Yes it confuses SAs also from time to time.
But that aside create at least one partition for Sorcerer
and then select that partition in the fstab menu
in order to fill out the mount point of /
and the filesystem type of ext3
After that it is done.
