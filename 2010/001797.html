Hi Kyle,

I typically run XFS and haven't experienced any problems with it on
Sorcerer. However, I'm a bit of a stickler to creating /dev/hda1 as a
primary boot partition of about 100MBytes, I stick to Lilo, and I format
that filesystem as Ext2.

But that's just me.

There are things you can't do with XFS, although it is superior in most
other respects to Ext4, which I almost never use (Google chose it over
XFS but said it was a very close call), one of those things being the
inability to shrink partitions (which is of no consequence to me).

The other filesystem I like is ReiserFS. Most of the decision in
determining on whether I'm going to go w/XFS or ReisferFS is based on
the average size of the files on that volume - one is better w/bigger
files, while the other one is better w/smaller files.

All things being the same, I choose XFS. Even if I no longer use Irix ;)

I just have always liked the fact that I can revert to an LCD (Least
Common Denominator) wrt filesystems if I have an issue, making it easy
to access my /boot on /dev/hda1.

Where I differ from Sorcerer, and where I had most of my dificulties,
was in creating a swap partition instead of the swap file in the
standard installation. I could be wrong, and might be creating extra
work for myself, but I like the idea of SWAP memory on its own actual
partition (Kyle, what are your thoughts on this?).

This I typically put this on /dev/hda2

regardless of the SWAP issue/prefs, perhaps Roelof could benefit from a
/boot on the primary, bootable partition of /dev/hda1 - I glean that
there is no support for booting / as XFS from the Sorcerer I/R ???

Dunno if something like Knoppix supports that, as I have a custom
Slackware CD I spun to support such a mount wrt XFS.
