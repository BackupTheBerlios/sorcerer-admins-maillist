> Excellent. I figured that was the issue you were having ;)
> 
> This all started back when HDDs exceeding 1024 cylinders needed a
> workaround. So we started creating a /boot on an /XdX1 primary, bootable
> flagged partition.
> 
> SGI boxes were RISC machines, not CISC boxes, similar to how Sun boxes
> boot, so it wasn't an issue for XFS until people started wanting to make
> / an XFS filesystem on x86 machines.
> 
> I've never cared much for Ext3 or Ext4 filesystems, although in truth it
> is just because I haven't really given them a chance and started using
> other filesystems before they were introduced.
> 
> Ext2 had/has a filesize limit of I believe 2GB, which, gave me problems
> when I started mastering the .COM zone myself for failsafe reasons years
> ago. Once that zonefile reached the limit of what Ext2 could accommodate
> I had to look for another filesystem to use, and since I was already
> using a special partition to accommodate /boot I never ran into the
> issue which slammed you.
> 
> Nowadays, most folks don't understand the significance of a /boot on its
> own partition, but there are nevertheless still a lot of good reasons
> for the implementation of that practice :)
> 
> Sorry that you ran up against this, but hey, you got some kewl practice
> in Sorcerer installs eh?
