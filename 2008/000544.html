Way to go Gáspár Ákos !!!
:)

That is what I want to see,
SAs honing their BASH skills.

As Gáspár Ákos demonstrated,
a tiny 3 to 5 line sequence of BASH commands
is often all that is required to accomplish a task.

The limit of what can be accomplished is not:
Limit = sorcery * SA skill.
The equation is:
Limit = sorcery ^ SA skill.

For SAs that are still novice with writing BASH scripts,
the time spent honing will be more than regained
by being able to write eloquent fast executing
BASH scripts and being able to
add new augur command modules whenever curiosity beckons.

I apologize if the changes
to augur frustrate Sorcerer SAs.
The desired outcome is for:
Sorcerer SAs bash skills to exceed 1.
There is an excellent feeling of elation,
that I can not explain in words,
that happens when SAs bash skills reach a certain ability.
Sorcery becomes less like a tool and more like a paradigm.

Here is an analogy....
Please do not sue me Lucas Arts.  :)
If Sorcerer SAs were "jedi knights" and "sith lords"
then BASH would be the "force" and sorcery would
be a "light saber."

But in staying with the Sorcerer theme...
sorcery would be the way to power,
the grimoire holds the words of power,
and the ability of a SA to channel and wield
this this awesome power would be BASH skill.

There is a history of computer evolution that
follows a trend to separate the users of apps
and programs from the programming.
Sorcerer development does not follow this trend.
Is sorcery an atavist or merely a different evolutionary path?

Like any distro Sorcerer will fail to do everything that
a senior level SA requires from it.
However, with sorcery's excellent bash code
and use of simple text files for data/logs
SAs can more easily program solutions
that achieve the desired outcome.

sorcery as a tool is as deficient as any
system administration tool found in any distro.
However, as a paradigm it offers much more
than other distros have.
Sure a few nifty features and functionality pioneered
in Sorcerer are nice fluff.
But sorcery as a paradigm is the true boon,
that empowers SAs to accomplish
more customization for less effort.

I hope this helps clarify the reasons for recent changes.
As usual the one step back is painfully apparent
while the three steps forward is not nearly as obvious.
