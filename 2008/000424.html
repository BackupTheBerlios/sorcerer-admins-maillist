Sorcerer libselinux FAQ:

Q: Does having libselinux installed make a box selinux enabled?
A: No

Q: Does having libselinux installed adversely
effect a non selinux enabled box?
A: No

Q: Why is libselinux installed on non selinux enabled boxes?
A: Because the transition to SELinux is easier
when the software that can require libselinux are
already compiled and linked with libselinux

Q: Is Sorcerer forcing SAs to deploy SELinux enabled boxes?
A: No. When booting linux-modular
a kernel parameter can completely disable SELinux support
selinux=0

Q: Can I augur exile libselinux and related spells it installs?
A: Absolutely.

Q: On a non SELinux enabled box
what difference does having libselinux installed make?
A: # ls -lah /var/cache/archive/libselinux-i686-1.34.15.tar
600K

libselinux is now like alsa-lib
Even if the box does not require it
it is probably installed.
Less carefully deployed Sorcerer boxes already had it installed.
And having libselinux installed reduces
the amount of questions about optional requirements
therefore expediting a SA's progress
through the hundreds of menus.

In the past Sorcerer was among the most
lean non embedded distributions available.
By using:
# augur exile
# dispel --unsafe
a Sorcerer box can still be stripped down
to below sane and safe deployments.

The base deployment of Sorcerer until 2008 was a shack.
Cozy and small was nice.
Required some time and effort to deploy.
However, when the security improvements are complete
the base Sorcerer deployment will be a castle
with an optional keep at the center.
Overall it will be a happy compromise between
a bare box and a fortress.
Think of libselinux as your new unmanned guard tower.  :)
Feeling better about?
