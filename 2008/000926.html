Okay, maybe you did what Evert was anticipating.
Perhaps, you transferred files and then continued
down the disk menu and selected to unmount partitions.
If the partitions are unmounted then the compile menu
is either not available on the main menu
or it can not be entered.

It is just a really tiny mistake that anyone could make.
I am glad that you reported it because it reinforces
Evert's idea of a single menu as being a good idea.
I want to give it a try and run with it for at least a
few releases of the I/R to see what SAs say about it.

A wizard is not out of the question.
However, it might implemented as a separate installation script.
That way SAs have their choice between cake or pie.
But best to finish the cake before making the pie.

The newer I/R will have a nearly large single main menu design
that should help SAs avoid the potential pitfall of unmounting
the filesystems before compiling required software.

> Perhaps more wizard like could be better? Like all others distros does

> selecting menus, etc. thats confusing.

Senior level SAs, and especially me, tend not to like to be cornered.

to go back any step and make corrections it was abandoned.
