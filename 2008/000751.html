There are 3 init-scripts installed by init-scripts that merit consideration:

/etc/init.d/tmp
/etc/init.d/usr_src_sorcery_bind
/etc/init.d/usr_src_sorcery_tmpfs

The above list is also the order of execution at runlevels: 1 2 3 4 5

tmp  mounts 3g tmpfs on /tmp
*_bind  binds /tmp/.sorcery to /usr/src/sorcery
*_tmpfs  mounts 3G tmpfs on /usr/src/sorcery

Make any of these executable or non-executable.
The correct decision may prolong disk life.

If all 3 are executable
then tmp would be 3G
and /tmp/.sorcery would bind to /usr/src/sorcery
and tmpfs would not be mounted on /usr/src/sorcery

If all 3 are non executable
then mounting of /tmp and /usr/src/sorcery can be controlled by /etc/fstab

Choose any combination of on and off.
All 3 do not have to be concurrently on or off.

Unless previously adjusted, the default is:
on  /etc/init.d/tmp
on  /etc/init.d/usr_src_sorcery_tmpfs
off /etc/init.d/usr_src_sorcery_bind

This change adds greater flexibility to box configuration,
and simplifies the overly complicated /etc/init.d/tmp
which previously did both tasks of /etc/init.d/tmp and
/etc/init.d/usr_src_sorcery_bind

I apologize for any inconvenience this may cause.
Most SAs can ignore this email and their boxes will
receive a suitable 3G tmpfs on /tmp and /usr/src/sorcery
Back it with enough RAM and swap if you intend to fill it.

If concerned about users creating a denial of service
by running a box out of memory by filling /tmp
then a smaller /tmp can be specified in /etc/fstab
while /etc/init.d/tmp is made non-executable.
