Haha.
I often wondered if I won you over to using linux-modular.
It really is a nice spell.

However, in response to reading what Evert way saying about
using linux, because he likes a custom configuration also,
I was doing some testing to see if an athlon-xp specific kernel
non SMP works any faster than the SMP linux-modular generic.
And I could not tell any difference.
However, I did do some other reading here and there
and learned that the
"BIOS Spread Spectrum" clock modulation to reduce
EMI of a specific frequency emission is supposed to
have a slight performance cost.
And also that this other option, what it was called now I do not exactly recall,
turns off the clock signal to RAM slots where not RAM is installed
which reduces EMI...
It was automatic something.
Anyway to make a long story short
overclocking produces better performance improvement
than the difference between a precisely optimized kernel
when compared to linux-modular.
However, that is not at all an endorsement or recommendation
for anyone to overclock their box.
Overclocking is not wise.
However, it is nice to know that the hardware makers
allow for better performance in their CPU RAM and PCI cards
than is precisely required for normal non overclocked configurations.


Sounds like a rainy day is on the way.
