Jan has some very good ideas for improving repository support
> and might have a prototype in testing.
> He was suggesting that entire repositories be kept in
> /var/cache/sources/repo/$SOME/$THING
> and that tarballs can be rolled from the repository and places in
> /var/cache/sources/$SPELL/$VERSION
> By that fashion sorcery would still be tarball based
> however it would also be able to update repositories stored locally
> in /var/cache/sources/repo/ instead of downloading it new each time.
> He also had some ideas for supporting download by rsync.
> I also am interested in seeing how it turns out.


I am also interested. However, I disagree with the placement of the
repositories.
I would rather see /var/cache/svn/$SPELL
and /var/cache/cvs/$SPELL
and /var/cache/git/$SPELL
and /var/cache/rsync/$SPELL
and then roll the required sources with dates in to /var/cache/sources as
tarballs.

Reasons behind this.
1. I have always considered /var/cache/sources to be a repository for source
tarballs.
2. The way repositories work, you only need one for each spell, and can go
either backwards or forwards in time as you choose.
3. Smaller, more descriptive absolute directory names in scripts which are
easier to troubleshoot.
