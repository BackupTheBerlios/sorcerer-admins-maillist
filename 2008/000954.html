Thanks for the HowTos.
However, depending on one's taste it may simply be easier
just to specify the desired modes in /etc/X11/xorg.conf
It may be almost inescapable if using NVIDIA-* with dual monitors
since there must be some way to specify position and whether
the desktop is duplicated or extended across both monitors.

What I dislike is having a virtual screen display larger than the
monitor resolution.
Even if I use kdm's control panel to switch to lower resolutions
I end up with a large virtual display than physical display.
Wait, that is called virtual in xorg.conf, right?

I have a vague recollection of starting an X server without
an xorg.conf at sometime and ending up with 1280x1024 resolution.
More resolution is nice, but it does not look as good on my monitors.
I prefer the higher refresh less flickering and so forth.
Yes I still use CRTs on all of my computers and not LCDs.

Thanks again for the URLs.
I agree that it is good that there is more than one way to configure X.
For example pointer buttons can be assigned or re-assigned using
/etc/X11/xorg.conf or it can be done from one's own ~/.xsession
by invoking xmodmap
It provides a good compromise for giving both SAs and users
the X(perience) that they desire.  *chuckle*  Yes, a little pun intended.

Oh, it may be worth noting that if someone has an old enough monitor
there is the potential that starting X without specifying an maximum mode
may cause the monitor to pop, break, or otherwise damage.
Although it was not due X
I did somehow wreck a monitor more than a decade ago.
Fortunately, it was still under warranty.

Anyway, if the monitor is a CRT I always specify in
/etc/X11/xorg.conf the horizontal frequency range
and vertical frequency range.
It is usually either printed in the monitor manual book
or on a sticker on the back of a monitor.
