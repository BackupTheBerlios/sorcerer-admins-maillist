I sync pretty much three times a week.

As such, I run in to issues every now and then, and report it back.
Then again, I am not running Sorcerer on mission-critical machines.

What I have been clamouring for for a while is sorcerer-stable and
grimoire-stable
The idea behind these spells would be that we test the hell out of a
grimoire and sorcery for a period of time, and then call it stable. Then we
can go ahead and change the innards of the normal Sorcery and grimoire as
much as we want, test it again and again until satisfied that it works
properly, and update grimoire-stable and sorcery-stable.

Twice a year sounds like a good interval for doing the stable testing, and I
would not mind doing the re-compiles and testing for this idea to work.

People running sorcerer-stable and grimoire-stable would be behind the
bleeding edge, but still have all the power and ease of Sorcery at their
finger tips. These spells would also be a good starting point for a Sorcerer
I/R, as all the spells on there are known to work at their respective
versions.

Kyle, do you think that it is a good idea to roughly twice a year freeze
feature updates on Sorcerer, and announce a bug-hunting month in which we
aim to get all of the spells in Sorcerer playing nice and compiling in order
to make sorcerer-stable and grimoire-stable possible?

I am sure that the Sorcerer System Admins out there would love to help with
the bug reports and testing involved.

Kind regards,
-Evert Vorster-
