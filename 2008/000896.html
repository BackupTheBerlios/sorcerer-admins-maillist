> Hi there.
>
> Hmm... I like leveraging the power of Sorcery, and have already done some
> creative things with augur progress.
>
> Like, I have some spells that I have svn versions of. So, I have a little
> script that updates from svn and puts the compressed source in the cache
> with $SOURCE-svn in the cache. Then, with augur progress $SPELL svn, I can
> cast the svn version.
>
> Granted, there is room for improvement, such as specifying the exact date of
> the svn that I want. Still, using the Sorcery tools beats the pants off of
> trying to do it all by myself.
>
> Once a month might be a bit frequent for stables, Kyle.
> I happily volunteer some time spent compiling all the sources I have
> installed and troubleshooting all the compile errors about a month before
> each I/R release. More frequent than that and it starts feeling like work.
>
> Also, my life cycle does not quite permit a monthly ... anything, really. I
> spend five weeks on board a ship, and the laptop that I run Sorcerer on is
> running incredibly light in the spell division, with only about 500 spells
> installed. I have not installed anything but KDE4. Anything that requires
> KDE3 libs is simply not installed and I try to manage without it. Then I go
> home and might only have a few weeks there, but a much more powerful machine
> and many, many more spells installed.
>
> So, for me at least, trying to keep to a monthly schedule simply does not
> fit. However, if I know a I/R is coming up, then I can juggle my time a
> little and re-compile everything and give you reports on what fails and why.
>
> I suppose the I/R is the closest thing we have that would equate to a binary
> distro's release and it only makes sense to try and get those as stable as
> possible.
>
> When I say stable, I mean compiles without errors, of course. The actual
> stability of running the software can only be sorted out by actually running
> the software. Most my spare time is now spent editing video, and testing
> openmovieeditor svn. Currently it is the only bit of linux software that can
> edit the avchd video that my camera produces, so it makes it worth it to get
> that nice and stable and tested fully. The libraries that depends on are
> also getting put through the wringer, and bug reports go to the various
> maintainers.
>
> Hopefully at some time in the future I can come back to you and say that the
> following versions of whatever has been tested, tested again, bugs have been
> squashed and it works well for me.
> Considering the sheer amount of spells in the grimoire, it is humanly
> impossible to do testing on each and every one, so I only comment on the
> spells I actually use.
>
> Kind regards,
> -Evert-
