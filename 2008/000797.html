Boxes can now skip the BIOS POST and bootloader menu
and boot directly into the most recently installed kernel and initramfs when
# shutdown -r now
is issued and if kexec-tools is installed

I forgot what SA contributed the spell for kexec-tools.
That person deserves the thanks.
However, until recently I had not made the time to
read kexec docs, create two init-scripts for kexec-tools,
and modify init-scripts installed by the init-scripts spell
so that the init-scripts installed by kexec-tools
are properly ordered for execution.
However, now that is complete
having installed kexec-tools is no longer dead weight.

Some boxes might reboot faster.
On my boxes I barely notice any difference.
However, kexec rebooting should be more gentle on hardware
since it is neither a cold nor warm boot.

For boxes frequently boot into other operating systems
not having a bootloader menu would be an inconvenience.
Consequently, kexec-tools will not be installed automatically.
There is no spell requirement upon kexec-tools.
If kexec style rebooting is desired then
# cast kexec-tools.
No configuration files require modification.
