Fascinating.
However, do not panic.
The bug report is made by people who
almost never boot to a new kernel.
touch on ia32 and x86_64 architectures
from coreutils 6.12 on Sorcerer booted with
linux-modular version 2.6.25.5 works.

I am uncertain how old of a linux kernel one must run
in order to encounter the error.
The easy solution is to reboot boxes with outlandish
uptimes before updating to the new coreutils.

I would expect that the coreutils authors
would avoid using something
that has been available in the kernel
for less than 3 months.

If you are not booted with a version +2.6.25
please do so before updating coreutils.
For such a rare bug I am not adjusting the spell.
