The /lib64 and /usr/lib64 directories were a bad idea to begin with.
The majority of free software authors do not support it.
Some programs will not work when their files are moved */lib to */lib64
Some libraries require adjustments to their libtool and pkgconfig
files in order to be moved.
Not every source supports --libdir=/usr/lib64

Therefore, what you have for Sorcerer x86_64 is
very much what the original software authors intended
and not something where I am trying to force libraries
to be installed in lib64 directories.

Even today I am doing compilation in qemu-system-x86_64
I might not have enough CPU cycles to keep the I/R current.
It is currently compiling gcc-v4.2 with 6 more spells waiting.

Because I do not run Sorcerer x86_64
and do not do compile testing on x86_64
I rely upon Jan to make x86_64 appropriate fixes to spells
whether it be to make them compile or to install into lib64 directories.
And I have not instructed anyone to try to force lib64 directories.

If I had an x86_64 box
then Sorcerer x86_64 would be a better port.
For now, if you report problems
then you will receive fixes.
The fix that went out in yesterday's grimoire
that has NVIDIA-Linux-x86 remove MesaLib libGL libraries
should fix the OpenGL problem.
I wish we had that fix the first moment
I wrote the NVIDIA-Linux-x86 spell.
But I have never cast that spell on x86_64, nor tested it on x86_64.
I merely fix it according to bug reports.
