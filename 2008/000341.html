As you may have already noticed.
Some of the init-scripts previously installed by spells such as
e2fsprogs and util-linux are now installed by a single spell called
init-scripts.
Usually, I will not allow one spell to collect and install init
scripts from other spells.
However, in this instance the benefits outweigh the potential confusion.

init-scripts now has a configure()
that does something I have wanted for a while.
It presents a checklist of init-scripts.
Those init-scripts are those which can be optionally executed during
boot and shutdown.
