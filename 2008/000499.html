Very true.
However, having /etc/X11/xorg.conf can still be useful.
enabling DRI, setting DRI mode,
setting up the pointer device, resolutions and depths,
loading modules, etc...

However, there is one thing that I am most happy about.
modelines are not required in /etc/X11/xorg.conf
I became very happy when mode lines became optional,
because finding proper mode lines for some monitors
and resolutions was not as easy as looking on the back
or in the manual.

Anyway, having an /etc/X11/xorg.conf that mostly works
to begin with is easier than launching X and then trying to
tweak the or adjust the mode after X is launched.
I do no longer recall the name of the utility that does that.

I wonder if anyone actually launches X without a configuration file?
I hate having a view port that is larger than the physical resolution,
except when changing X mode with ctrl-alt-+ and ctrl-alt-minus
to zoom in, however I rarely do that anymore.
