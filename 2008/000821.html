I knew someone who bought a 486DX 66Mhz and ran it
for half a year in slow speed with caches disabled and
he never knew because he never went into the BIOS CMOS menu to check.
However, when I gave his box an inspection I adjusted BIOS CMOS
and improved his computer's performance by more than 400%
And that was without any over clocking.
I merely corrected the BIOS CMOS from factory defaults
to what they should properly be.

Definitely, a misconfigured BIOS CMOS can cost an enormous amount
performance and can reduce performance to merely 10% of optimal.
Performance loss may be even more applicable to notebooks which
can throttle down the CPU to 12.5% of maximum speed.

Therefore, I agree that proper tuning is easily worth a few hundred
dollars compared to running a modern computer at 1/3 full speed.

It gives cause to wonder how many computers people are using that
have sub optimally configured BIOS CMOS?
As far as I know linux does not do much if anything to correct it.
However, at least CPU throttling can be set by echoing values to
somewhere in /proc
and MTRRs are properly programmed for
AGP and PCI Express graphics cards by xorg.
