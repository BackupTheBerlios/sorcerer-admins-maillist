Thanks for the report.
I will look into the
System->Screen resolution
application problem to see if it
is also missing for me.
I would expect that it may be due
to the content of /etc/X11/xorg.conf
not having a subsection of Section "Screen"
such as:

    SubSection     "Display"
        Viewport    0 0
        Depth       24
        Modes      "1024x768" "800x600" "640x480"
    EndSubSection

Without alternate modes specified in
/etc/X11/xorg.conf
there may not be any choices for a
different resolution than what X
decides to start with.

Since I like the screen's appearance better in
1024x768 than 1280x1024 I use modes to
specify that I do not want 1280x1024 since
it might be selected automatically if I let
X make the choice.
My preference is do to the ancient CRT monitors
that I use and not font size.  :)

Then again, there may be something missing
from the gnome installation on that box
which is required to adjust screen resolution?
However, I recommend checking
/etc/X11/xorg.conf
because it fast to check and fast to fix.
