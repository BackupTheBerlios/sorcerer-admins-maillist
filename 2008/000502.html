For a long while I have contemplated sorting the sorcery logs into two
categories
that determine to what location they should install.

For example:

Logs: install; id; elf; version; md5sum; config
should still be installed in /etc/sorcery/log/
They are essential log files.

However,
Logs: compile; snapshot; etc; event; activity and
such can be removed without dire consequence
and could be installed in /var/log/sorcery/

Obviously, that sort of transition will lighten /etc/sorcery/log/
and bulk up /var/log/sorcery/
But is that a useful boon?
Are there any banes for this sort of change besides
having new SAs confused about whether the logs they want
are in /etc/sorcery/log/ or /var/log/sorcery/ ?

Since essential logs would not be moved but instead stay in /etc/sorcery/log/
there should be no potential for loss of important logs.
