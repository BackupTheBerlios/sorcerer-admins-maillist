Switched to a nVidia Corporation NV44A [GeForce 6200] for my workstation.
It has one big heat sink and no fan.
The dual GPUs, one for 2d and one for 3d should ensure nice low temperature
for almost 100% of the time since I rarely run OpenGL.
I recall writing that I would buy no more NVIDIA nor ATI.
But for $50 and it drives 2 monitors and has no fan to melt I figured why not.
Also it has a temperature monitoring and reporting.
Compared to the 9800 PRO that required an additional power cable to operate
the NVIDIA card makes the 9800 PRO look like kindling.
With my sort of luck with selecting graphics cards, mother boards,
hard disk, etc...
I expect the odds are slim that it will last 90 days.
But I will try to be optimistic about it.
Perhaps it will last 91 days.
I wonder if I may yet miss that warm glow from the 9800 PRO on cold
winter nights?
I added my xorg.conf to NVIDIA-Linux-x86.d/
If anyone has a problem setting it up, perhaps due to not specifying a
ModulePath
then I already know that configuration works for 2 two monitors.
