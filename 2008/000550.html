Okay interesting... I have, from time to time, had issues with linux
complaining such as 'unable to mount 0803' you know... when lilo.conf
doesn't point to the right root= device?...

well this linux modular kernel is doing the same thing... apparently
root=0803 is passed in /proc/cmdline... and init fails and prompts for
root password.  So, having now gained a LOT more knowledge of this
whole boot process, I was able to mount /dev/sda2 on /media/root and
re-exec /init... then the system came alive[ish].

So... I rebooted, and booted passing the root=/dev/sda2 to the kernel
as a lilo option (from the lilo boot screen) and the system came up
without complaints....

how do I convince lilo to not pass a 'funky' number and pass the name
specified instead?  (/dev/sda2)
