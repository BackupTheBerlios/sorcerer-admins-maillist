Thanks for the response.
When I try to speak for other Sorcerer SAs
I tend to sound audacious and presumptuous.
I can only state with certainty what I want for Sorcerer.

I want to make compromises
that make Sorcerer easier to use for new SAs.
Yet obviously Sorcerer's niche can not be
both senior level SAs and new SAs.
I anguish myself because of that.
Sorcery and Sorcerer are good tools
that every SA aught to be able to use.

Yet piloting a Sorcerer box is a bit like piloting an aircraft or automobile.
A moderate amount of training or experience is useful if not essential.
Integrating some of the training into the experience
of installing Sorcerer could be a good idea.
Yet, I have a difficult time making such changes
in a way that does not compromise something that
existing Sorcerer SAs want to preserve.

I have tried other methods.
I tried using DirectFB instead of X in order to provide a gtk+ installer
and having an installed video player output to DirectFB.
Although DirectFB has been under development
it might not work on all hardware and might not work as expected.
And even such changes as adding DirectFB and gtk+
to the Install/Rescue increase the size.

My previously stated idea of having an I/R
that downloads additional archives from the Internet
in order to boot as a live CD was perhaps the best
compromise I have envisioned thus far.
That is because the improvements
do not increase the size of the Install/Rescue disk.
Yet unless anyone wants to wait 20 minutes for it to boot
then it remains an idea that is discussed yet unimplemented.

Compared to a distro that offers a live CD then some SAs
might decide that Sorcerer is deficient.
That is one perspective.
Yet are there any 200M or smaller live CD
with all the Xorg, KDE, vlc and fluff?  *lol*
That is the converse perspective.
The truth is that Sorcerer is good at what Sorcerer is good at.
No distribution is good at everything.
That is why each distro has it's own distinct flavor
that will not satisfy every SA that tries a taste.

As for additional IRs....
I am trying to roll as few as possible,
so I can make them as perfect as possible.
Yet, I might have to re-introduce the i686 uni-processor I/R
that has a kernel which does not require a CPU that supports the PAE
instruction.
Buddy requested a non PAE kernel.
I am astonished anyone still has a PentiumM CPUs.

A change to 3 I/Rs per release, requires 1.5x more testing.
To have separate live CD I/Rs for each one creates 2x more ISO9660 images.
If I must repeatedly roll and test 6 I/Rs, 3 of them jumbo sized,
then when do I have time to do anything else?

If live CD I/Rs are created then it must be a compromise that
does not increase the amount of ISO9660 images
that must be rolled, tested, and uploaded.

Doing a release of new I/R every 4 to 6 months requires about a week's worth
of effort in order to test and perfect 2 I/R images.
Even something like the "file" command changing the output from:
"xz compressed data"
to
"XZ compressed data"
costs half a day to properly track and fix in sorcery and the I/Rs.
Nearly undetectable changes in the way programs work
or their output typically requires detection, revision, and another
round of testing.

The ISO9660 images that I upload are not merely expected to work.
They are tested and demonstrated to have worked.
With that much attention to detail
no one should be surprised when I state that
I am unwilling to create 3 additional jumbo sized live CD I/Rs.
Six ISO9660 images per release
that consume a total of nearly 3G of disk space
is not going to happen.

I would enjoy discussing the time when
I wanted to make 40 gigabyte
credit card sized Install/Rescue CDs.
But that is another story.  :)

>> No matter how we look at the situation
