On 06/02/2011 05:16 AM, Kyle Sallee wrote:

> 
> Okay, so remember to acquire current Install/Rescue images.
> And remember to deploy boxes with at least 2G ext2 for /boot
> when the box requires a /boot partition.

Yes we hashed this out here on the list a little less than a year ago,
due to issues, in particular, with XFS.

What I happened to notice on one host I use a lot, when I was browsing
it's phpSysInfo
(http://cardinal.lizella.net/psi/index.php?disp=dynamic), was that its
/boot filesystems is formatted as ext3, instead of ext2

Of course, with just 2GBytes or 3Gbytes, I' very comfortable with an
ext2 on /boot, trust that I can recover from bad things using muscle
memory that goes back almost two decades, etc., yet I'm wondering if
there are any actual advantages/disadvantages to using ext3 or otherwise
on /boot.

Maybe it's time that I look at ext3/4 for /boot? yes/no?

bear in mind, I hardly ever have to deal with e2fsck anyway because I
deploy mostly servers (my apologies, but someday I'll get around to
deploying a Sorcerer workstation when I can make it all the way through
to Xfce or KDE).
