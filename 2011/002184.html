I liked the last install I did, with one exception. It told me what I
needed to do as far as run fdisk, create your partitions, swap, choose
your mount points, etc.

Great for the seasoned SA, who is worried he/she might forget a step,
but knows how to do it with a couple of nice reminders. This is usually
my choice on an install, to, "Tell me what I need to do and in what
order, and let me do it myself".

Something unique, that I like in what you've done, is to enforce a
minimum of 2GB for a /boot - but only where it's required (i.e., not
with ext3 or ext4).

Now, for the problem I had, which (and this might sound a little stupid
on my part) by default, wanted to create an /etc/fstab with a *swap
file*, instead of mounting SWAP on it's own type 82 partition.

I had never done that before, and the thought of it actually makes me
cringe. I have, for the past 18yrs or so, always created a type 82
partition, and put my swap there.

In my case, I had to run sinstall twice before I figured out how to do
that in one step. Once to get through the fdisk and fstab part of the
install, and once to point choose the now existing partition as the
place to mount swap.

That might sound silly, but I still do not understand why someone would
want a swap file, instead of a swap partition.

Okay, moving on. From what it sounds like (and I'll create a new VM in
vmware  to see it for myself in a few minutes here), the requirement now
is that an SA *must* create a /boot of *at least* 2GB "if" he chooses
something like xfs, which needs one - perfect!
