Hi Kyle,

# augur update grimoire

instead of

# augur begin grimoire

actually seems more intuitive (to me at least). In the reasoning behind
your changing this particular nomenclature, is it because you have
future plans for an *augur update* in some other respect?

Thanks,

Bradley.



On 05/25/2011 08:12 PM, Kyle Sallee wrote:
> The reason for having the separate augur commands for:
> # augur sync
> # augur newer
> # augur update
> was understood only by SAs
> that understood the significance
> of the "grimoire," "sorcery," and other installed "spells."
> 
> However, not all SAs want to understand.
> They wanted to execute
> a single command updates
> all the installed software on a box.
> That was why I eventually created
> # augur easy
> 
> However, after a significant elapse of time
> I decided that I am no longer satisfied with
> the implementation of
> # augur sync
> 
> Consequently, I redesigned
> # augur sync
> to replace
> # augur easy
> 
> And hindsight suggests that I should
> have implemented it that way from the start,
> although I never use
> # augur easy
> 
> # augur sync
> now updates all installed software on a box.
> 
> This seems easier to grasp than
> having several commands
> used when updating a box.
> 
> The next anticipated question is...
> "What command updates only
> the installed grimoire and installed sorcery?"
> 
> # augur begin grimoire
> 
> # augur newer
> is still available.
> However, most SAs will not invoke it.
> Additionally, it queues spells with a current
> time stamp instead of a future time stamp.
> 
> # augur easy
> is deprecated.
> However, it will still invoke
> # augur sync
> for 6 months.
> 
> # augur update
> is deprecated.
> However, it will still invoke
> # augur begin
> for 6 months.
> 
> Deprecated augur command module
> are removed after 6 months.
> 
> # augur configure was modified.
> Previously, it configured spells that
> were provided as command line parameters,
> that were provided on stdin,
> or were already queued in the cast queue.
> However, it now also grants complimentary
> configuration for all installed software.
> 
> Here is a simple example.
> Let us assume that the aumix spell is already installed.
> 
> # augur forget opts aumix; augur configure
> 
> augur presents a question
> allowing confirmation or denial
> of the use of gtk+ during the compilation of aumix.
> 
> Therefore,
> # augur configure
> enables a SA to both check and configure
> all installed spells and gain assurance that
> no pending configuration questions
> involving installed spells are left unanswered.
> 
> However, there might be only one spell
> in the entire grimoire that is unable to cast
> without the SA first answering configuration questions.
> That spell would be linux-custom
> Therefore, what sorcery does not know
> will normally not hurt it.
> It will conjecture for itself which optionals to enable
> when the SA has not supplied a previous answer.
> 
> And now for the summary.
> # augur sync
> replaces the functionality of
> # augur easy
> 
> # augur update
> and
> # augur easy
> will entirely vanish in 6 months
> 
> # augur begin grimoire
> is an alternative to
> # augur easy
> in order to prevent additional spells
> from being added to the cast queue.
> 
> These changes better approximate
> how augur grimoire and sorcery
> should have been implemented years ago.
> However, I am always hesitate about changing
> the code and interface that involves
> how boxes are updated.
> That is why I originally implemented
> # augur easy
> as an entirely separate command
> instead of revising
> # augur sync
> That eliminated the probability that adding
> # augur easy
> would break
> # augur sync
> # augur newer
> and
> # augur update
> 
> However, I am now accepting the risk
> of derailing Sorcerer boxes in order
> to make updating an easy concept
> for new Sorcerer SAs to learn and accomplish.
> 
> I hope that everyone can be happy about this change.
> Nothing is being sacrificed,
> except for potential confusing concerning
> which commands should be executed
> in order to update installed software.
> 
> # augur sync
> 
> That command alone is sufficient.
> Remember that the action happens in the background.
> So follow with
> # augur scry
> to enjoy the show.
> 
> I apologize in advance for any boxes that
> I accidentally derail.
> Best to check that boxes are still updating automatically
> or as expected in a week from now.
> 
> These changes will require approximately two days
> before they reach most Sorcerer boxes.
> Because I tend to derail updating of Sorcerer boxes
> approximately once every 3 to 4 years
> please remain vigil because
> there is no more probable time that now
> for me to derail the distribution.
> And I will try to be very careful to not break it.
