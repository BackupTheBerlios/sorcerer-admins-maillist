Ah.

It's only a small annoyance, and actually Kyle has addressed most of
my complaints with the latest incarnation of /usr/sbin/lilo.

I do a lot of system installations, and as such I do run lilo more
often than most.

I'm used to just typing "lilo" at the command line, and this would
update the bootblock with the setup in /etc/lilo.conf

Previous to this last incarnation of /usr/sbin/lilo, there was a
symlink to /etc/init.d/lilo, and just typing "lilo" at the command
prompt would more often than not not write the bootblock, since the
sorcerer scripts did not see anything change.

So, my only point remaining is the lilo utility has some expected
behavior. To be puritan about it, we really should be calling the
/usr/sbin/lilo something else, as an SA experienced with lilo might be
surprised with the outcome of just typing "lilo" at the command line
and seeing quite a bit more execute than just lilo writing the boot
block.
