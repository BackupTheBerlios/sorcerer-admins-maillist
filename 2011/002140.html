On Wed, Apr 27, 2011 at 9:22 PM, Kyle Sallee <kyle.sallee at gmail.com> wrote:
> While helping Nathan Jeffrey Allen
> set up wireless networking
> I was was searching for firmware for a wireless adapter
> and stumbled across something I wish I knew about a couple years ago.
>
> http://kernel.org/pub/linux/kernel/people/dwmw2/firmware/
>
> Someone is finding free and non-free firmware required by linux kernel drivers
> and tossing it into a git repository and rolling it into a tar.bz2.
> Excellent!
>
> I am creating a spell for it called linux-firmware.
> It will come pre-installed on the next set of Install/Rescue disks.
> This should further minimize or eliminate the possibility of a new
> installation lacking required firmware for necessary drivers.
> And it also allows me to cross off my partially complete audit of
> linux's Kconfig files while looking for required firmware and websites.
>
> The drawback is the initial spell's implementation is no file shaving.
> So each initramfs and the amount of RAM required for booting
> will increase by about 30M.
> The bane is a 30M larger initramfs and larger I/Rs,
> but the boon is less of a problem with missing firmware.
> This makes the boon to bane an even decision.
>
> However, I am going to favor the new spell,
> because in this situation I was searching for firmware
> for the brcm80211 module and nothing in the Kconfig file
> suggested that firmware was required.
> Also I failed to discover a homepage for the firmware by using a google search.
> However, the firmware is included in the linux-firmware tarball.
> And the tarball contains plenty more firmware
> for devices that I know nothing about.
> So linux-firmware seems like the best bet for a firmware availability solution.
