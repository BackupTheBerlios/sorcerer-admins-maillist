Perhaps a 'little' off topic, but I'm having a few *issues* with
terminology here.

Okay, very few of us outside the realm of Ham radio still say, "KC's",
instead of KiloHertz (KHz), and even then we really only do that when
talking about spinning the dial so many, 'KC's'.

Everywhere else we talk about KHz's and MHz's and GHz's....

Are we supposed to be saying things like KiHz's and MiHz's, etc....?

What's up with this: http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IEC_60027 ????

I mean, 10^6 is still 2^20 !!!

And KiB, instead of KB or KBytes???

WTF are we supposed to call a *nibble* now? (an ibinib, LOL!?)

And is a Byte, now an ibiByte? Or a bit now an ibibit?

OMFG!!!!

Somebody wanna weigh in here on why this is productive, relevant, and
how I might get my head around this *deprecation* so I can justify and
embrace this new reference terminology?
