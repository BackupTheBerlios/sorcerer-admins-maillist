> So Evert, when I go and look at the btrfs sites everything I read
> indicates it's more of something akin to a 'beta in flux', state, and
> there are caveats suggesting it shouldn't be used in production machines.

It will eat your data.
But then so will a failing disk or bad controller. Once you have a
good backup schedule going you don't have to be afraid of any
filesystem.

So far, I have not had data corruption, and there are no reports of
such on the mailing list. I had lost the entire filesystem a few
times, but only because of bad shutdowns, and then not every time I
had a bad shutdown, either... just on the cheap disks.

So, why do I use it, then?

Well, ease of use is a great factor. It's manpage is about one page
long, and explains everything.

It's quite zippy. Since my filesystem is considered to be very fragile
in any case, I run raid0 and backup often. With three disks capable of
120mb/s sustained throughput, I get over 300mb/s sustained, and 3gb/s
bursts.... :)

It's easily expandable, and once you get your head around it, makes a
lot of sense.
