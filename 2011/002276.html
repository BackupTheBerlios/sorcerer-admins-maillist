I invariably used noatime for over a decade.
Perhaps like many SAs I was ignorant of relatime,
failed to notice when the linux default switched to relatime,
and was bewildered why file atimes were not updating as I expected.
relatime is a compromise between atimes and noatimes.
It reduces the amount of atime writes
by updating atime sloppily.

Consequently, immune-boot-blaze will temporarily mount the monitored
file systems with strictatime to restore traditional behavior.
After the observation phase duration elapses
then the file systems are remounted with relatime.
This enables immune-boot-blaze to properly locate
the files that were accessed during the first 15 minutes of uptime.

I am providing this additional information
to clarify that no special modification of /etc/fstab
is required in order to receive the benefit of immune-boot-blaze.
