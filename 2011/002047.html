Okay now we've crossed one of those proverbial *lines* that I rant on
about - The RH/Slack line.

As you know, I've been a Slackware fan since about 93 (It being better
than Jolix or SLS or MCC, and marginally better than Yggdrasil at that
time).

I frisbee'd my way out of the Redhat camp at the end  of version 5.2 -
one of the main reasons was the introduction of sucky releases beginning
w/6.0 and 7.0 (Like mACROsFOT, you had to wait to at least a .1 release
before it was stable).

My take on the matter is that you can install a Redhat (or other RPM
based distro) box and spend 'x' amount of time disabling all the holes
and daemons you shouldn't be running or exposing your machine with, or
you can spend an inverse (somewhat less) amount of time *enabling* that
which you want from a basic Slackware install.

I'm [almost] *always* going to want the "little seed" and take the time
to grow it right.

I don't like pruning things - it leaves a mess and things laying around
in what should be empty space.

For example, I'm simply not going to install X on a machine I'm
purposing for NAS - it just ain't gonna happen - so why should I be
forced to remove that, or the httpd server, or the ftp server, etc.?

I know what I want, and there shouldn't be any other ports open on that
box but the ports servicing requests for that requirement.

So with that in mind... Why would I want to do an install twice? Once to
install the OS and the other actually being a sort of *uninstall*, where
I spend half the day 'dispelling'.

Man - just nmap an RH, Fedora, or CentOS box out of the gate, LOL!

> There is a difference between required for initial installation

Having a *choice* between *expert* (cli) install and GUI install in this
way doesn't detract at all, IMO, from the philosophy, utility, and
superiority of Sorcerer Linux.
