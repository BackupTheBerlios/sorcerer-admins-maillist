> My guess is that they are most likely
> involving spells that install only header files.
> In those instances an augur lddof spell_name
> would not detect spells requirements that are
> not of an ELF library nature.
>
> The last time I did tests were the initial cast of xorg
> on a completely fresh deployment of Sorcerer.
> Unfortunately, I did not detect any problems.
> It could be in some cases that a potential problem
> may be hidden merely due to the difference in order
> of which spells being or finish compilation on different boxes.
> Sorcerer does not enforce a strict order, but rather an order
> that requires only explicitly related spells to compile and
> install in order.
> So if at no point in a requirement chain
> does spell A requires spell B nor does spell B require spell A.
> Then, either spell B or spell A could be cast first depending
> upon which already has a source downloaded for it.
> However, if all the required sources were already downloaded,
> which was the case with my testing
> then the order that spells A and B would cast in would be specific
> to the order in which they appear in /root/.sorcery/index/order
>
> I hope that is not upsetting.
> However, knowing that fact may help identify why
> cast xorg may work flawlessly on one box, but could
> potentially trip on an unexpectedly required but not
> spell that provides required header files for compilation.
> So if you encounter it, please report specifically what spells are
> involved.
> To circumvent add in those requirements if they are obvious,
> but if not, download all the sources for spells with
> proto as part of their name, regenerate the index,
> and restart the cast of xorg.
> That will probably step around any problems
> involving required xorg header files not being installed.
