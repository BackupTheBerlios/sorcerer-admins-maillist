It is true that there are some areas that are not of interest
during augur alien.

However, that does not mean that files and directories
whether intentional or unintentional do not exist there.
Here is an example:

Let us say that a someone accidentally rebooted a box during an update.
/tmp/.sorcery is --bind to /usr/src/sorcery
And /tmp is a real filesystem.
When the box finishes rebooting the directory and files
of that spell that was casting previously might still exist.
Now they are just wasting space.
It may be a week before sorcery decides to auto clear that directory.
Or it might exist even longer if I did not code a check for that.

So on one hand a SA doing augur alien may not be interested in files
that exist in /usr/src/
On the other hand if there was a stale directory from 2006
in /usr/src then the SA might want to go ahead and remove it.

The easiest way to exclude files from the output of augur alien
is to pipe it through grep:

# augur alien | grep -v "^/etc/sorcery/\|^/usr/src/" | tee /tmp/aliens.txt

Now the output to /tmp/aliens.txt will not show aliens
within the two directories specified above.
Let us say after a quick review of /tmp/aliens.txt
the SA is not interested in aliens that exist in /etc

# sed -i "\,^/etc/,d"  /tmp/aliens.txt

The above command removes lines beginning with /etc/
from the file /tmp/aliens.txt

Now the list of aliens is smaller.

I recommend creating a wrapper in /usr/local/bin
that executes # augur alien | grep -v ....
that parses down the list to what you typically want to search.
