To diagnose the problem, it would be helpfull if you send us the compile log 
of gcc when it failed, or at least the last part of the log where the error 
is. Use augur compile gcc to review the log, or for example, 'augur compile 
gcc | tail -n 50' to get only last 50 lines. The full log is 
in /etc/sorcery/log/compile/gcc.

And dispelling gcc is a bad idea. If you have it installed, don't dispel it or 
you may end up with seriously broken system. And if you have it installed, I 
would assume that means the compilation (cast) succeeded, didn't it? 

Show us the log and we will be able to help better.
