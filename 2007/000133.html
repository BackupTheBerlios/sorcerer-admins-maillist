leech creates a searchable hard link index of the files in /var/cache/sources
Depending on what it decides it must do...
that might take a no time, a little time, or a significant amount of time.
If leech is running, but not curl,
then you might want to see if leech has invoked find or grep.
You may find /usr/bin/pstree to be an amusing method for seeing
how processes are related to each other.

Sorcerer boxes with immensely stocked /var/cache/source/
directories are known to have an observable delay between when
# leech $SPELL
is issued
and when the actual download begins.
A delay can also be attribute due to CPU load, or load from cast,
or leech could be waiting for the right time to download the spell's sources.
In the event that other spells are being leeched or waiting to leech
the spell requested to be leeched may have to wait to be processed.

When leech is invoked with a spell name
it places that spell appropriately into the leech
queue before leech re-executes itself as a daemon
to do the actual downloading of the sources for spells.
Consequently, there should not be a race condition.
