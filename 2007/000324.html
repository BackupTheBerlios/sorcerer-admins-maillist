After glibc version 2.7 failed to compile on the i586 architecture
I decided to see how far along uClibc has come towards approximating glibc.
After 3 days of testing I was impressed with uClibc.
However, putting aside any concern for the numerous amount of patches required
to compile important software such as gcc, there is still one near
insurmountable problem.
ELF backward compatibility of the library is not yet robust enough to
seamless update a box with ELF uClibc libraries in place of glibc.
Perhaps in the future we can have more choices about what C library to use.
At least for a while the prospect of using uClibc should wait until it
has both better backward compatibility and better support for uClibc
becomes present in vanilla gcc and other vanilla sources.
